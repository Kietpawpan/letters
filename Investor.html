Intelligent Investment

Dear Son,

If you want to invest in common stocks, learn to be an intelligent investor first. Never be a speculator.

Before you buy any stocks, make sure you are an intelligent investor, or you will be risky of loosing the money.

You should have read the book by Ben Graham, The Intelligent Investor. At least, read the two chapters recommended by Warren Buffet:

  • Chapter 8 The Investor and Market Flucuations
  • Chapter 20 Margin of Safety as the Central Concept

Besides, you should have studied the bussiness behind that stock. It must be a good business, and its current stock price should be fair. Do not buy a fast-growing stock, nor should you buy an overly priced stock. You can check whether it is a good business with a fair stock price or not, by using the jTech-TEXAS calculator. Then, your should develop a mathematical model of that business, to falsify a theory of its expected long-term earning.

Finally, it is crucial not to listen to anyone about which stock to buy or to sell. Believe in the findings from your own careful experiment. You will be safe and can sustain your prosperity.



Love,

Dad Tony
